# Nibli — Book-vs-Code Audit Backlog

This backlog records the gaps found by auditing the source tree against the book in `book/`. Overall fidelity is high: the parser, semantic compiler, reasoning core, WASM/WIT surface, and Transparency Triad broadly do what the book describes, and the deterministic firewall verdict is sound in the common cases. The highest-priority remaining theme is the two shipped case-study corpora (`gdpr.lojban`, `drug-interactions.lojban`) not actually encoding the scenarios their chapters walk through. (Now fixed: the `logji` reasoning-soundness defects — negated rule conditions evaluated as positive, predicate-cache poisoning, and proof steps claiming `holds:true` with no derivation — and the smuni relative-clause firewall guarantee, which now rejects ambiguous implicit-`ke'a` clauses rather than silently dropping the binding; and the gerna forethought biconditional `go A gi B`, which now parses and lowers to a biconditional.) A long tail of medium/low items covers performance regressions (per-node rebuilds on the hot path), documentation-vs-code mismatches (Appendix C WIT signatures, package name, Ollama temperature, back-translation glosses), and pervasive DRY duplication that has already caused traced/untraced evaluator drift.

## Priority summary

HIGH-severity items, critical work first:

- **drug-interactions.lojban does not contain the warfarin/fluconazole/CYP2C9 scenario the chapter is built around** (case studies) — the chapter's only worked example cannot be reproduced from the shipped file.
- **gdpr.lojban does not encode the consent / Article 6-7-17 reasoning chain the chapter walks through** (case studies) — the belief-revision demonstration cannot be reproduced from the shipped file.
