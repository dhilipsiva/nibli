# Nibli — Book-vs-Code Audit Backlog

This backlog records the gaps found by auditing the source tree against the book in `book/`. Overall fidelity is high: the parser, semantic compiler, reasoning core, WASM/WIT surface, and Transparency Triad broadly do what the book describes, and the deterministic firewall verdict is sound in the common cases. Both case-study corpora are now fixed. (Fixed: the `gdpr.lojban` corpus — a maximal, reproducible Article 5/6/7/9/15/17/33 compliance KB with the consent-withdrawal belief-revision chain, entities renamed to `la .adam.`/`la .akmes.`, and parse + end-to-end + belief-revision regression tests; Chapter 20 rewritten with captured engine output and Big-4 service-line framing. A long tail of medium/low items covers performance regressions (per-node rebuilds on the hot path), documentation-vs-code mismatches (Appendix C WIT signatures, package name, Ollama temperature, back-translation glosses), and pervasive DRY duplication that has already caused traced/untraced evaluator drift.
